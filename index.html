The High Top Design

The High Top of the Converse was invented for ankle support by basketball player Chuck Taylor It has become extremely beneficial to Converse's usuabilty and provides mroe visual interest. This sillouette is original to the brand since 1917.

The high ankle support was noticed by skateboards and was then on adopted into a skating shoe with the support and durabilty supported by other features, Converse began to reach new horizons with demographics

"Chuck Taylor" Star Patch

The iconic Converse patch was not just a statement piece, it was created to prevent basketball players from knocking their ankles together. This was a design choice made by Chuck Taylor, his signature was added to the shoe making it the first celebrity endorse athletic shoe in the world. The patch soon became an iconic symbol for the shoes, becoming easily recognisable providing the brand with originality and cultural significance.

Stripped Outersole

The Outer sole of the converse was deisgned and made for durability and grip. Coverse were made to last, in fact, the first pair of Converse ever made is still in complete tact!

Converse shoes can be seen as an investment especially due to the thick outersole which on the chuck 70's are made out of CX rubber which is designed for even further flexabilty further adding to the quality of our best selling shoe.

Rubber Toe Cap

The original design for the toe cap was for protection and style in order to minimise the impact basketballers had on impact and wear. This toe cap evolved into a much beloved skatebaording shoe as it was tough enough to handle to condition of the skateboard and pavement.

Now it is easily distinguishable from competitors and adds to the iconic appearance.

Rubber Sole Shoe

In the early 20th century rubber soles were frequently used to make durable, flexible and waterproof shoes. Converse are authentic to their roots and have kept the rubber soles since the original shoe.

The Rubber sole also makes for a more comfortable base gaining the versitlity of everday wear.
